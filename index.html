The Rappahannock is the lifeblood of central Virginia. River boats run daily, carrying passengers and sightseers alike up and down the river. Long home to indigenous peoples, the Rappahannock region has played a crucial role in American history. Traversing 195 miles, it flows east through northern Virginia before emptying into the Chesapeake Bay.

The majestic Blue Ridge Mountains - the river's origin - are a short ride west, perfect for immersing yourself in the state's natural beauty. Hiking, camping, and skiing are just a few of the activities Virginians practice, drawing tourists from around the country.

Did you know Virginia is one of America's most prolific wine producers? Our world-class wines start their journey in the humble vineyards that roll across central Virginia. Many wineries offer tastings in this under-the-rader viticulture destination.

An hour's drive south of the Rappahannock brings you to Richmond, Virginia's diverse and dynamic capital. Home to world-class universities and museums, Richmond is the beating heart of Virginia's unique culture. From fine dining to professional sports and live music, Richmond will keep you inspired throughout your stay in Virginia.
